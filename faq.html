Files & Design

We accept the following formats:
  • STL — the most common format for 3D printing
  • STEP / STP — preferred for engineering parts with precise geometry
  • OBJ — good for complex organic shapes
  • 3MF — includes colour and material information
STEP files are often preferred as they preserve exact geometry. STL files should be exported at high resolution (deviation ≤ 0.05 mm).
For best results:
  • Ensure the model is watertight (no holes or non-manifold geometry)
  • Set units correctly — we work in millimetres
  • Keep wall thickness above 1.2 mm for FDM, 0.4 mm for resin
  • Avoid unsupported overhangs greater than 45° where possible
  • If tight tolerances are required, specify them in your notes

Materials & Printing

Our FDM material range includes:
  • PLA — general-purpose, great detail, wide colour range
  • PETG — more durable than PLA, good chemical resistance
  • ABS — heat-resistant, impact-resistant, machinable
  • ASA — UV and weather-resistant, suited to outdoor use
  • TPU — flexible and rubber-like
  • Engineering filaments — nylon, PC, carbon-fibre blends for demanding applications
We also offer resin (SLA/MSLA) printing for high-detail parts. Not sure which material suits your application? Include a description of how the part will be used in your quote request and we'll advise.
FDM (Fused Deposition Modelling) builds parts by melting and depositing filament layer by layer. It's great for functional parts, structural components, large prints, and a wide range of engineering materials. Layer lines are visible but can be sanded smooth.

Resin (SLA/MSLA) cures liquid photopolymer resin with UV light. It produces very fine detail and a smooth surface finish, making it ideal for miniatures, jewellery masters, dental models, and display items. Resin parts are generally more brittle and less heat-resistant than FDM engineering materials.
Typical tolerances for FDM printing are ±0.2 mm for standard prints. Tighter tolerances (±0.1 mm) may be achievable depending on part geometry, material, and print orientation — please specify your requirements in the quote request so we can advise. Resin printing typically achieves ±0.05–0.1 mm.
We offer the following post-processing services:
  • Support removal and surface clean-up
  • Sanding (medium and fine grades)
  • Priming and spray painting
  • Heat-set insert installation
Please request any post-processing at the quoting stage so we can include it in the price.
